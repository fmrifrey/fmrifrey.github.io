David Frey

PhD Student at the University of Michigan

Welcome to my personal website. I'm a PhD student at the University of Michigan studying novel acquisition and reconstruction techniques for silent functional MRI at the Functional MRI Laboratory under the supervision of Dr. Doug Noll. I also have previous experience working with perfusion imaging via arterial spin labeling (ASL) under the supervision of Dr. Luis Hernandez-Garcia, and magnetic resonance fingerprinting (MRF) under the supervision of Dr. Jesse Hamilton during my PhD rotations at MIITT. My interests include k-space trajectory optimization and model-based image reconstruction techniques, particularly for non-cartesian dynamic MRI. In my free time, I also coach and compete in olympic weightlifting!
